Efficiency tradeoffs
 The large-scale anatomical network of the macaque monkey can be computationally re-wired to minimize wiring cost (top panel; left side) but only at the expense of increasing path-length (bottom panel; left side). 
Similarly, the connectome of C. elegans can be rewired to minimize connection distance (top panel; right side), but path-length in both local and global networks is increased as a result (bottom panel; right side)
The global efficiency of functional networks derived from human functional MRI data increases monotonically with increasing connection density (top panel).
